# NCLEX Prep Nation — Brand Identity and Visual Direction Guide

## 1. Document Purpose

This document defines the brand identity, design style, colors, typography, tone of voice, messaging, logo direction, UI style, trust language, and CTA language for NCLEX Prep Nation.

The purpose is to make sure the website looks professional, trustworthy, medical, educational, and conversion-focused.

Every designer, frontend developer, content writer, and AI coding assistant must follow this guide.

---

## 2. Brand Name

**NCLEX Prep Nation**

---

## 3. Brand Meaning

The name NCLEX Prep Nation communicates more than exam preparation.

It suggests:

- A preparation community
- A student support system
- A place where candidates prepare together
- A platform with structure and guidance
- A serious exam readiness environment

The word “Nation” should make students feel they are joining a supportive preparation community, not just buying a file.

---

## 4. Brand Positioning

NCLEX Prep Nation is positioned as:

> A trustworthy NCLEX preparation community that helps nursing candidates prepare with original practice questions, detailed rationales, NGN-style case studies, study resources, mock exams, progress tracking, and guided support.

The brand should be seen as:

- Independent
- Ethical
- Professional
- Student-focused
- Exam-focused
- Helpful
- Structured
- Supportive

---

## 5. Brand Mission

The mission of the brand is:

> To help nursing candidates prepare for NCLEX with clear study direction, original practice questions, detailed rationales, and guided support.

---

## 6. Brand Vision

The vision is:

> To become a trusted online NCLEX preparation community where students can practice, learn, track progress, and receive support with confidence.

---

## 7. Brand Promise

Recommended brand promise:

> Prepare with clarity. Practice with purpose. Build confidence for NCLEX.

The website should not promise guaranteed passing unless a legally approved guarantee policy exists.

Avoid false promises such as:

- Guaranteed pass
- 100% success
- Real NCLEX questions
- Secret exam questions
- Pass without studying

## 10. Tone of Voice

The tone should be calm, clear, supportive, and professional.

### 10.1 Good Tone Examples

Use language like:

- “Start with a free diagnostic test.”
- “Review detailed rationales after each question.”
- “Identify your weak areas before choosing a study plan.”
- “Request access and our team will guide you through the next step.”
- “Practice with original NCLEX-style questions.”
- “Build stronger clinical judgment through case-based practice.”

### 10.2 Bad Tone Examples

Avoid language like:

- “Guaranteed NCLEX pass!”
- “Get real NCLEX questions now!”
- “Secret questions exposed!”
- “Pay before the offer disappears!”
- “Only smart students use this!”
- “Failing is your fault if you do not buy now!”

---

## 11. Brand Messaging Pillars

The brand should communicate through five major pillars.

### 11.1 Trust

The website must show that the platform is legitimate, ethical, and independent.

Message:

> We provide original preparation resources and do not sell real or leaked exam questions.

### 11.2 Structure

The website must show that students will receive a clear preparation path.

Message:

> Know what to study, where you are weak, and what to practice next.

### 11.3 Practice

The website must focus on active practice, not just reading notes.

Message:

> Practice with NCLEX-style questions, rationales, and NGN-style case studies.

### 11.4 Progress

The website must make students feel they can track improvement.

Message:

> Monitor your scores, weak topics, saved questions, and readiness.

### 11.5 Support

The website must show students that they can request human guidance.

Message:

> Request materials, contact us through WhatsApp/email, or choose guided support.

## 12. Color Palette

The visual identity should use a medical, educational, and trustworthy color system.

### 12.1 Primary Color: Deep Medical Navy

- Hex: `#0B1F3A`
- Use for: header, footer, major headings, dark sections, sidebar, admin navigation
- Meaning: trust, professionalism, authority, seriousness

### 12.2 Secondary Color: Clinical Blue

- Hex: `#1769AA`
- Use for: primary buttons, active links, icons, highlights
- Meaning: healthcare, education, calm confidence

### 12.3 Accent Color: Healing Teal

- Hex: `#1BAE9F`
- Use for: progress bars, success states, checkmarks, positive badges
- Meaning: healing, progress, care, calm support

### 12.4 Background Color: Soft Ice Blue

- Hex: `#F3F8FC`
- Use for: page backgrounds, section backgrounds, light card areas
- Meaning: clean medical learning environment

### 12.5 Text Color: Charcoal

- Hex: `#1F2937`
- Use for: paragraph text, labels, readable content
- Meaning: strong readability

### 12.6 Muted Text Color

- Hex: `#6B7280`
- Use for: subtitles, helper text, small descriptions

### 12.7 Border Color

- Hex: `#E5E7EB`
- Use for: cards, input fields, separators

### 12.8 Success Color

- Hex: `#16A34A`
- Use for: correct answers, completed modules, payment confirmed, progress completed

### 12.9 Error Color

- Hex: `#DC2626`
- Use for: incorrect answers, form errors, warnings

### 12.10 Attention Color

- Hex: `#F59E0B`
- Use for: reminders, pending payment, follow-up needed

## 15. Logo Direction

The logo should be simple, clean, and professional.

### 15.1 Logo Concept

Recommended logo ideas:

1. Text logo with a small shield icon
2. Text logo with a checkmark and book icon
3. Text logo with a stethoscope and open book
4. Text logo with a medical cross and progress check
5. Text logo with a shield representing trust and preparation

### 15.2 Logo Text

Use:

> NCLEX Prep Nation

Possible layout:

- “NCLEX” bold
- “Prep Nation” normal or medium weight

### 15.3 Logo Rules

Do:

- Keep it clean
- Use brand colors
- Make it readable on mobile
- Create dark and light versions
- Use simple iconography

Do not:

- Use official NCLEX/NCSBN logos
- Copy Pearson VUE identity
- Use complex medical symbols
- Use too many colors
- Make it look like a hospital logo only

### 15.4 Required Logo Variants

The project should include:

1. Full horizontal logo
2. Stacked logo
3. Icon-only logo
4. White logo for dark backgrounds
5. Dark logo for light backgrounds
6. Favicon

## 23. Writing Rules

### 23.1 Use Simple Language

Good:

> Practice by topic and review detailed rationales after every answer.

Bad:

> Our advanced cognitive review model provides a premium remediation ecosystem.

### 23.2 Use Honest Claims

Good:

> Designed to support NCLEX preparation.

Bad:

> Guaranteed to make you pass.

### 23.3 Use Student-Centered Language

Good:

> Not sure where to start? Take a diagnostic test and see your weak areas.

Bad:

> Our system is the best in the world.

### 23.4 Use Clear Manual Payment Language

Good:

> Submit your package request and our team will contact you by WhatsApp or email with the next steps.

Bad:

> Payment API is not available yet.

- Make practice questions readable.
- Keep navigation simple.
- Avoid heavy animations.
